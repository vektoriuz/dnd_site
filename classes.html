Classes Overview

Classes define your character’s primary role, abilities, and progression. They determine how you approach combat, interact with the world, and grow over time.

Below, classes are organized by complexity — from straightforward and accessible to mechanically demanding and flexible. Within each group, they are ordered from least magic-focused to most magic-focused.

Beginner-Friendly Classes

Barbarian

Mode: Melee

Role: Tank / Carry

Complexity: Low

A direct, durable frontline fighter focused on rage-based survivability and physical damage. Minimal resource management and very clear decision-making.

Fighter

Mode: Melee / Ranged

Role: Carry / Tank

Complexity: Low

A flexible weapon-based class with strong action economy. Straightforward to play, but with room for optimization through positioning and timing.

Intermediate Classes

Rogue

Mode: Melee / Ranged

Role: Carry

Complexity: Medium

Precision-based damage dealer relying on positioning and timing. Rewards planning and awareness of battlefield conditions.

Paladin

Mode: Hybrid (Melee + Magic)

Role: Tank / Carry / Support

Complexity: Medium

A durable frontline class with burst damage and limited spellcasting. Balances offense, defense, and support capabilities.

Ranger

Mode: Hybrid (Ranged + Magic)

Role: Carry / Utility

Complexity: Medium

A versatile tracker and sustained damage dealer with light spell support. Focuses on consistency and environmental interaction.

Warlock

Mode: Hybrid (Caster + Sustained Attack)

Role: Carry / Utility

Complexity: Medium

A unique caster with limited but frequently refreshed spell slots. Combines reliable ranged output with selective magical abilities.

Cleric

Mode: Hybrid (Melee + Magic)

Role: Support / Tank / Damage

Complexity: Medium

A highly flexible class with access to healing, damage, and utility. Prepared spellcasting adds adaptability but increases decision complexity.

Advanced Classes

Monk

Mode: Melee (Mobile)

Role: Carry / Control

Complexity: High

A fast, mobile striker that relies on resource (Ki) management and positioning. Requires careful planning to maximize effectiveness.

Sorcerer

Mode: Caster

Role: Carry / Control

Complexity: High

A focused spellcaster with limited spell selection but powerful customization through metamagic. Efficiency and planning are key.

Bard

Mode: Hybrid (Magic + Support)

Role: Support / Control

Complexity: High

A versatile support class with strong utility and control options. Requires understanding of many tools and when to apply them.

Expert-Level Classes

Druid

Mode: Hybrid (Caster + Shapeshifting)

Role: Control / Support

Complexity: Very High

Combines a wide spell list with shapeshifting mechanics. High flexibility but requires managing multiple systems simultaneously.

Wizard

Mode: Caster

Role: Control / Carry

Complexity: Very High

The most expansive spellcaster, with a large spellbook and preparation system. Extremely powerful but demands deep system understanding.
