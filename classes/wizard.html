Wizard

A master of arcane knowledge who prepares spells from a vast spellbook to control and reshape reality.

Wizards are the most versatile spellcasters, with access to the largest range of spells. They rely on preparation, planning, and deep system knowledge to adapt to any situation, making them extremely powerful but demanding to play.

Overview

  • Primary Role: Control / Carry
  • Combat Style: Full Caster
  • Complexity: Very High
  • Key Abilities: Intelligence

Wizards are defined by their breadth of options. Their effectiveness depends heavily on choosing the right spells and preparing for expected challenges.

Core Traits

  • Primary Ability: Intelligence
  • Hit Point Die: D6 per Wizard level
  • Saving Throw Proficiencies: Intelligence, Wisdom
  • Skill Proficiencies: Choose 2: Arcana, History, Insight, Investigation, Medicine, Nature, Religion
  • Weapon Proficiencies: Simple Weapons
  • Tool Proficiencies: None
  • Armor Training: None
  • Starting Equipment: Choose A or B: (A) 2 Daggers, Arcane Focus (Quarterstaff), Robe, Spellbook, Scholar's Pack, and 5 GP; or (B) 55 GP

Core Mechanics

Spellcasting (Prepared from Spellbook)

Wizards maintain a spellbook containing a large number of spells. Each day, they prepare a subset to use, allowing for high adaptability with proper planning.

Spellbook

A growing collection of spells that can be expanded over time. This creates long-term progression and customization.

Arcane Recovery

Allows you to regain some spell slots after a short rest, providing limited resource recovery.

Level 1 Features

  • Spellcasting — Access to arcane magic
  • Spellbook — Core system for storing spells

At level 1, the Wizard begins with a defined set of spells but quickly expands their capabilities as their spellbook grows.

Playstyle

In Combat

  • Control the battlefield with powerful spells
  • Target multiple enemies or key threats
  • Stay at a safe distance and avoid direct damage

Resource Management

  • Spell slots must be carefully allocated
  • Prepared spells determine your available options
  • Planning ahead is critical for effectiveness

Out of Combat

  • Provides unmatched utility through spell variety
  • Can solve problems in multiple ways depending on preparation

Subclasses (Arcane Traditions)

Wizards choose a tradition that defines their specialization in magic.

  • School of Evocation — Focuses on direct damage spells
  • School of Illusion — Specializes in deception and manipulation
  • School of Abjuration — Enhances defense and protection
  • School of Divination — Gains foresight and control over outcomes

Each tradition enhances a different aspect of spellcasting, allowing Wizards to specialize while maintaining overall versatility.

Strengths & Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Largest spell selection in the game
  • Exceptional control and versatility
  • Can adapt to almost any situation

Weaknesses

  • Very fragile compared to other classes
  • Highly dependent on preparation and knowledge
  • Resource management is demanding

Building a Wizard

Ability Score Priorities

  • Intelligence — Core spellcasting ability
  • Constitution — Improves survivability
  • Dexterity — Helps with defense

General Advice

  • Prepare spells based on expected encounters
  • Think ahead rather than reacting in the moment
  • Position carefully to avoid danger
