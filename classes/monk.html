Monk

A fast and precise martial artist who uses inner energy to enhance mobility, defense, and combat techniques.

Monks rely on speed, positioning, and resource management rather than raw durability. They excel at striking quickly, avoiding damage, and controlling the flow of combat through mobility and tactical decisions.

Overview

  • Primary Role: Carry / Control
  • Combat Style: Melee (high mobility)
  • Complexity: High
  • Key Abilities: Dexterity, Wisdom

Monks are defined by their ability to move freely across the battlefield while managing a limited resource pool. Their effectiveness depends on careful positioning and efficient use of abilities.

Core Traits

  • Primary Ability: Dexterity and Wisdom
  • Hit Point Die: D8 per Monk level
  • Saving Throw Proficiencies: Strength, Dexterity
  • Skill Proficiencies: Choose 2: Acrobatics, Athletics, History, Insight, Religion, Stealth
  • Weapon Proficiencies: Simple Weapons, Martial Weapons (Monk Weapons)
  • Tool Proficiencies: Choose 1: Artisan’s Tools or Musical Instrument
  • Armor Training: None
  • Starting Equipment: Choose A or B: (A) Spear, 5 Daggers, Artisan’s Tools or Musical Instrument, Explorer’s Pack, and 11 GP; or (B) 50 GP

Core Mechanics

Focus (Ki)

A resource used to activate special abilities. These include additional attacks, defensive maneuvers, and mobility options. Managing this resource efficiently is central to playing a Monk effectively.

Martial Arts

Allows you to make unarmed strikes and use Dexterity for attacks. Also enables additional attacks as part of your combat flow.

Unarmored Movement

Increases your movement speed, allowing you to reposition easily, engage or disengage quickly, and control distance in combat.

Level 1 Features

  • Martial Arts — Unarmed combat and bonus strikes
  • Unarmored Defense — Armor Class based on Dexterity and Wisdom

At level 1, the Monk establishes a fast, flexible combat style focused on movement and repeated strikes rather than single powerful attacks.

Playstyle

In Combat

  • Move frequently to control positioning
  • Use multiple attacks to apply steady pressure
  • Avoid staying in one place or taking prolonged hits

Resource Management

  • Focus (Ki) is limited and must be spent carefully
  • Decide between offense, defense, and mobility each turn

Out of Combat

  • High mobility allows effective scouting
  • Useful in physical challenges and traversal

Subclasses (Monastic Traditions)

Monks follow a tradition that defines how they channel their abilities.

  • Way of the Open Hand — Focuses on direct combat and control
  • Way of Shadow — Specializes in stealth and mobility
  • Way of the Elements — Uses elemental abilities to enhance combat
  • Way of Mercy — Combines offense with healing and support

Each tradition shifts the Monk toward either pure combat efficiency, stealth tactics, magical abilities, or supportive roles.

Strengths & Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Exceptional mobility
  • Flexible combat options
  • Strong control through positioning

Weaknesses

  • Lower durability compared to frontline classes
  • Relies heavily on resource management
  • Requires careful positioning to be effective

Building a Monk

Ability Score Priorities

  • Dexterity — Improves attacks and defense
  • Wisdom — Enhances abilities and defense
  • Constitution — Increases survivability

General Advice

  • Do not stay stationary — movement is your defense
  • Use resources where they have the most impact
  • Think in terms of positioning, not just damage
