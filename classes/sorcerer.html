Sorcerer

A natural spellcaster who channels innate magic and reshapes spells through internal power.

Sorcerers wield magic as an inherent ability rather than learned knowledge. They have access to fewer spells than other casters, but can modify and enhance them through unique abilities, making each spell more flexible and impactful.

Overview

  • Primary Role: Carry / Control
  • Combat Style: Full Caster
  • Complexity: High
  • Key Abilities: Charisma

Sorcerers are defined by efficiency and specialization. Their limited spell selection requires careful planning, but their ability to modify spells allows for powerful and creative use of magic.

Core Traits

  • Primary Ability: Charisma
  • Hit Point Die: D6 per Sorcerer level
  • Saving Throw Proficiencies: Constitution, Charisma
  • Skill Proficiencies: Choose 2: Arcana, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Persuasion, Religion
  • Weapon Proficiencies: Simple Weapons
  • Tool Proficiencies: None
  • Armor Training: None
  • Starting Equipment: Choose A or B: (A) Spear, 2 Daggers, Arcane Focus (crystal), Dungeoneer's Pack, and 28 GP; or (B) 50 GP

Core Mechanics

Spellcasting (Known Spells)

Sorcerers know a limited number of spells and cannot change them easily. This makes spell selection a critical long-term decision.

Sorcery Points

A resource used to fuel special abilities and modify spells. These points can also be converted into additional spell slots.

Metamagic

Allows you to alter how spells behave, such as increasing their range, targeting multiple creatures, or casting them more quickly. This is the defining feature of the Sorcerer.

Level 1 Features

  • Spellcasting — Access to innate magic
  • Sorcerous Origin — Defines your magical source

At level 1, the Sorcerer begins as a full caster with a defined magical identity, shaping their abilities and theme from the start.

Playstyle

In Combat

  • Use a small set of spells efficiently
  • Enhance spells with Metamagic for greater impact
  • Focus on positioning and timing

Resource Management

  • Balance Sorcery Points and spell slots carefully
  • Decide when to enhance spells versus conserving resources

Out of Combat

  • Provides utility through selected spells
  • Less flexible than prepared casters

Subclasses (Sorcerous Origins)

Sorcerers draw their power from a specific magical origin that defines their abilities.

  • Draconic Sorcery — Gains durability and elemental power
  • Wild Magic — Unpredictable and chaotic magical effects
  • Clockwork Sorcery — Focuses on order, balance, and control
  • Aberrant Sorcery — Gains psionic and otherworldly abilities

Each origin changes how the Sorcerer approaches magic, adding either reliability, unpredictability, or unique thematic mechanics.

Strengths & Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Highly efficient spell usage
  • Powerful spell customization through Metamagic
  • Strong burst and control potential

Weaknesses

  • Limited number of known spells
  • Dependent on resource management
  • Less adaptable than prepared casters

Building a Sorcerer

Ability Score Priorities

  • Charisma — Core spellcasting ability
  • Constitution — Improves survivability
  • Dexterity — Helps with defense

General Advice

  • Choose spells that remain useful in many situations
  • Use Metamagic to amplify key moments
  • Plan resource usage ahead of time
