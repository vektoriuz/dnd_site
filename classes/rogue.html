Rogue

A precise and opportunistic combatant who relies on positioning, timing, and skill to deal decisive damage.

Rogues specialize in exploiting openings rather than overpowering enemies directly. They excel at single-target burst damage, mobility, and skill-based interaction, making them effective both in and out of combat.

Overview

  • Primary Role: Carry
  • Combat Style: Melee or Ranged (precision-based)
  • Complexity: Medium
  • Key Abilities: Dexterity

Rogues are defined by their ability to convert positioning and timing into high damage. Their effectiveness depends less on raw stats and more on understanding combat flow.

Core Traits

  • Primary Ability: Dexterity
  • Hit Point Die: D8 per Rogue level
  • Saving Throw Proficiencies: Dexterity, Intelligence
  • Skill Proficiencies: Choose 4: Acrobatics, Athletics, Deception, Insight, Intimidation, Investigation, Perception, Performance, Persuasion, Sleight of Hand, Stealth
  • Weapon Proficiencies: Simple Weapons, Martial Weapons
  • Tool Proficiencies: Thieves’ Tools
  • Armor Training: Light Armor
  • Starting Equipment: Choose A or B: (A) Rapier, Shortbow, 20 Arrows, Leather Armor, 2 Daggers, Thieves’ Tools, Burglar’s Pack, and 8 GP; or (B) 100 GP

Core Mechanics

Sneak Attack

Deals extra damage when certain conditions are met, such as attacking with advantage or targeting an enemy engaged with an ally. This is the Rogue’s primary damage source.

Cunning Action

Allows you to take additional mobility actions (such as disengaging or hiding) as a bonus action, enabling flexible positioning every turn.

Expertise

Doubles proficiency in selected skills, making the Rogue exceptionally effective in specific non-combat tasks.

Level 1 Features

  • Sneak Attack — Conditional bonus damage
  • Expertise — Enhanced proficiency in selected skills

At level 1, the Rogue establishes its identity as a precision-based class that rewards awareness and tactical positioning.

Playstyle

In Combat

  • Position yourself to trigger Sneak Attack consistently
  • Focus on high-value targets
  • Avoid prolonged direct confrontation

Resource Management

  • No traditional resource pool — effectiveness depends on positioning
  • Each turn is about creating or exploiting an opportunity

Out of Combat

  • Excels at stealth, scouting, and skill-based challenges
  • Provides strong utility through Expertise

Subclasses (Roguish Archetypes)

Rogues choose an archetype that defines their specialization and approach to problems.

  • Thief — Focuses on agility, utility, and interaction with objects
  • Assassin — Specializes in ambush and high burst damage
  • Arcane Trickster — Adds limited spellcasting and magical utility
  • Soulknife — Uses psionic abilities for offense and versatility

Each archetype expands the Rogue’s toolkit, adding either tactical depth, magical options, or specialized roles.

Strengths & Weaknesses

Strengths

  • High single-target damage potential
  • Excellent mobility and positioning tools
  • Strong utility outside combat

Weaknesses

  • Lower durability compared to frontline classes
  • Damage depends on meeting specific conditions
  • Less effective in chaotic or crowded encounters

Building a Rogue

Ability Score Priorities

  • Dexterity — Improves attacks, damage, and defense
  • Constitution — Increases survivability
  • Intelligence or Charisma — Depending on playstyle

General Advice

  • Always aim to trigger Sneak Attack each turn
  • Use mobility to avoid unnecessary damage
  • Leverage skills outside combat to maximize value
