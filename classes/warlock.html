Warlock

A pact-bound spellcaster who gains power from an otherworldly patron, combining reliable offense with modular abilities.

Warlocks differ from other spellcasters by relying on a small number of powerful spell slots that refresh frequently. They balance consistent damage output with customizable abilities, making them both reliable and flexible within a constrained system.

Overview

  • Primary Role: Carry / Utility
  • Combat Style: Hybrid (Caster + Sustained Ranged Damage)
  • Complexity: Medium
  • Key Abilities: Charisma

Warlocks are defined by their efficiency and repeatability. Rather than relying on a large pool of resources, they use a consistent baseline of power combined with periodic bursts.

Core Traits

  • Primary Ability: Charisma
  • Hit Point Die: D8 per Warlock level
  • Saving Throw Proficiencies: Wisdom, Charisma
  • Skill Proficiencies: Choose 2: Arcana, Deception, History, Intimidation, Investigation, Nature, Religion
  • Weapon Proficiencies: Simple Weapons
  • Tool Proficiencies: None
  • Armor Training: Light Armor
  • Starting Equipment: Choose A or B: (A) Leather Armor, Sickle, 2 Daggers, Arcane Focus, Scholar’s Pack, and 15 GP; or (B) 100 GP

Core Mechanics

Pact Magic

Warlocks have very few spell slots, but they are always cast at the highest level and refresh on a short rest. This encourages frequent, impactful spell use.

Eldritch Blast

A reliable ranged attack that scales with level. It forms the core of the Warlock’s consistent damage output.

Eldritch Invocations

A set of customizable abilities that enhance spells, grant new powers, or modify core mechanics. These define much of the Warlock’s identity.

Pact Boon

A defining feature that grants a unique tool, such as a magical weapon, familiar, or additional abilities, further shaping your playstyle.

Level 1 Features

  • Pact Magic — Unique spellcasting system
  • Otherworldly Patron — Defines your source of power

At level 1, the Warlock establishes both its resource model and its thematic identity, which strongly influence all future choices.

Playstyle

In Combat

  • Use Eldritch Blast for consistent damage
  • Apply spells for key moments of impact
  • Maintain distance and positioning

Resource Management

  • Spell slots are limited but refresh frequently
  • Encourages regular use rather than conservation

Out of Combat

  • Invocations provide unique utility options
  • Strong thematic and roleplay presence

Subclasses (Otherworldly Patrons)

Warlocks form a pact with a powerful entity that grants them their abilities.

  • The Fiend — Focuses on offensive power and survivability
  • The Archfey — Uses charm, illusion, and control
  • The Great Old One — Gains psionic and mind-based abilities
  • The Celestial — Adds healing and supportive capabilities

Each patron shifts the Warlock’s focus toward damage, control, utility, or support, while maintaining the same core mechanics.

Strengths & Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Reliable, consistent damage output
  • Frequent spell usage due to short rest recovery
  • Highly customizable through invocations

Weaknesses

  • Very limited number of spell slots
  • Less flexibility than full casters
  • Dependent on core abilities for effectiveness

Building a Warlock

Ability Score Priorities

  • Charisma — Core spellcasting ability
  • Constitution — Improves survivability
  • Dexterity — Helps with defense

General Advice

  • Use your spell slots regularly, not sparingly
  • Build around your invocations and pact choice
  • Rely on consistent abilities between spell uses
