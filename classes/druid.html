Druid

A nature-bound spellcaster who controls the battlefield and transforms into powerful animal forms.

Druids are highly versatile spellcasters who combine control, support, and transformation. They can reshape the battlefield with magic while also shifting into animal forms to adapt to different situations.

Overview

  • Primary Role: Control / Support
  • Combat Style: Full Caster + Shapeshifting
  • Complexity: Very High
  • Key Abilities: Wisdom

Druids are defined by their flexibility and layered mechanics. They can switch between spellcasting and physical forms, making them adaptable but demanding to play effectively.

Core Traits

  • Primary Ability: Wisdom
  • Hit Point Die: D8 per Druid level
  • Saving Throw Proficiencies: Intelligence, Wisdom
  • Skill Proficiencies: Choose 2: Arcana, Animal Handling, Insight, Medicine, Nature, Perception, Religion, Survival
  • Weapon Proficiencies: Simple Weapons
  • Tool Proficiencies: Herbalism Kit
  • Armor Training: Light Armor, Medium Armor, Shields (nonmetal only)
  • Starting Equipment: Choose A or B: (A) Leather Armor, Shield, Scimitar, Druidic Focus, Explorer’s Pack, and 9 GP; or (B) 50 GP

Core Mechanics

Spellcasting (Prepared)

Druids prepare spells from a wide list, focusing on battlefield control, area effects, and utility. Their spells often shape the environment rather than dealing direct damage.

Wild Shape

Allows you to transform into animal forms with different abilities and statistics. This provides an alternative mode of combat, mobility, or utility depending on the form chosen.

Battlefield Control

Many Druid spells alter terrain, restrict movement, or affect multiple targets, making them highly effective at controlling enemy positioning.

Level 1 Features

  • Spellcasting — Access to nature-based magic
  • Druidic — Secret language of druids

At level 1, the Druid begins as a full spellcaster, with Wild Shape becoming available later, significantly expanding their capabilities.

Playstyle

In Combat

  • Control enemy movement and positioning
  • Use area effects to influence multiple targets
  • Switch to Wild Shape when physical presence is needed

Resource Management

  • Spell slots determine magical impact
  • Wild Shape uses are limited and situational
  • Choosing when to switch roles is critical

Out of Combat

  • Exceptional utility through spells and transformations
  • Can scout, traverse, and solve problems creatively

Subclasses (Druid Circles)

Druids choose a Circle that defines how they interact with nature and their powers.

  • Circle of the Land — Focuses on enhanced spellcasting and terrain affinity
  • Circle of the Moon — Specializes in powerful Wild Shape combat forms
  • Circle of the Sea — Gains abilities tied to water and fluid movement
  • Circle of the Stars — Uses cosmic power for support and ranged effects

Each Circle shifts the Druid toward either stronger spellcasting, transformation, or unique thematic abilities.

Strengths & Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Exceptional battlefield control
  • High versatility through multiple forms
  • Strong utility both in and out of combat

Weaknesses

  • High complexity and learning curve
  • Requires planning and system understanding
  • Less direct damage compared to some classes

Building a Druid

Ability Score Priorities

  • Wisdom — Core spellcasting ability
  • Constitution — Improves survivability
  • Dexterity — Helps with defense

General Advice

  • Think in terms of control, not just damage
  • Use Wild Shape strategically, not constantly
  • Prepare spells based on expected encounters
