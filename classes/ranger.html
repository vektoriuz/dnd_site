Ranger

Far from bustling cities, amid the trees of trackless forests and across wide plains, Rangers keep their unending watch in the wilderness. Rangers learn to track their quarry as a predator does, moving stealthily through the wilds and hiding themselves in brush and rubble.

Thanks to their connection with nature, Rangers can also cast spells that harness primal powers of the wilderness. A Ranger's talents and magic are honed with deadly focus to protect the world from the ravages of monsters and tyrants.

Core Traits

  • Primary Ability: Dexterity and Wisdom
  • Hit Point Die: D10 per Ranger level
  • Saving Throw Proficiencies: Strength, Dexterity
  • Skill Proficiencies: Choose 3: Animal Handling, Athletics, Insight, Investigation, Nature, Perception, Stealth, Survival
  • Weapon Proficiencies: Simple Weapons, Martial Weapons
  • Tool Proficiencies: None
  • Armor Training: Light Armor, Medium Armor, Shields
  • Starting Equipment: Choose A or B: (A) Studded Leather Armor, Scimitar, Shortsword, Longbow, 20 Arrows, Quiver, Druidic Focus (sprig of mistletoe), Explorer's Pack and 7 GP; or (B) 150 GP

Becoming a Ranger

Level 1 Character

  • Gain all the traits in the Core Ranger Traits table.
  • Gain the Ranger's level 1 features, which are listed in the Ranger Features table.

Multiclass Character

  • Gain the following traits from the Core Ranger Traits table: Hit Point Die, proficiency with Martial weapons, proficiency in one skill of your choice from the Ranger's skill list, training with Light and Medium Armor and Shields.
  • Gain the Ranger's level 1 features, which are listed in the Ranger Features table. See the multiclassing rules in multiclassing to determine your available spell slots.

Subclasses (Ranger Archetypes)

Chosen at level 3.

Hunter

Protect Nature and People from Destruction

You stalk prey in the wilds and elsewhere, using your abilities as a Hunter to protect nature and people everywhere from forces that would destroy them.

Beast Master

Bond with a Primal Beast

A Beast Master forms a mystical bond with a special animal, drawing on primal magic and a deep connection to the natural world.

Gloom Stalker

Draw on Shadow Magic to Fight Your Foes

Gloom Stalkers are at home in the darkest places, wielding magic drawn from the Shadowfell to combat enemies that lurk in darkness.

Fey Wanderer

Wield Fey Mirth and Fury

A fey mystique surrounds you, thanks to the boon of an archfey or a location in the Feywild that transformed you. However you gained fey magic, you are now a Fey Wanderer. Your joyful laughter brightens the hearts of the downtrodden, and your martial prowess strikes terror in your foes, for great is the mirth of the fey and dreadful is their fury.

Overview

  • Primary Role: Carry / Utility
  • Combat Style: Ranged or Melee (with light magic)
  • Complexity: Medium
  • Key Abilities: Dexterity, Wisdom

Rangers are defined by their consistency and flexibility. They deal steady damage over time while supplementing their combat with spells and environmental awareness.

Core Mechanics

Spellcasting

Rangers have access to a limited selection of spells focused on enhancing attacks, mobility, and utility. Their spellcasting supports rather than defines their role.

Favored Enemy / Hunter Features

Rangers gain abilities that improve their effectiveness against certain targets or enhance their combat style, reinforcing their identity as specialists.

Exploration Utility

Many Ranger abilities improve tracking, movement, and interaction with the environment, making them particularly effective outside of combat.

Playstyle

In Combat

  • Maintain consistent damage over multiple turns
  • Use positioning to control distance from enemies
  • Enhance attacks with spells when needed

Resource Management

  • Spell slots are limited and should support key moments
  • Balance sustained output with occasional boosts

Out of Combat

  • Excels at tracking, navigation, and environmental awareness
  • Provides utility through skills and spells

Strengths & Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Consistent damage output
  • Strong versatility and adaptability
  • Useful both in and out of combat

Weaknesses

  • Limited spellcasting compared to full casters
  • Less burst damage than some martial classes
  • Can feel unfocused without clear build direction

Building a Ranger

Ability Score Priorities

  • Dexterity — Primary combat stat
  • Wisdom — Supports spells and abilities
  • Constitution — Improves survivability

General Advice

  • Choose a clear combat style early (ranged vs melee)
  • Use spells to enhance your strengths, not replace them
  • Leverage your utility outside combat
