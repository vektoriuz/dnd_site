Ranger

A versatile hunter who combines weapon skill, mobility, and nature-based magic.

Rangers specialize in sustained damage, tracking, and adaptability. They blend martial combat with limited spellcasting, allowing them to respond to a variety of situations while maintaining consistent pressure.

Overview

  • Primary Role: Carry / Utility
  • Combat Style: Ranged or Melee (with light magic)
  • Complexity: Medium
  • Key Abilities: Dexterity, Wisdom

Rangers are defined by their consistency and flexibility. They deal steady damage over time while supplementing their combat with spells and environmental awareness.

Core Traits

  • Primary Ability: Dexterity and Wisdom
  • Hit Point Die: D10 per Ranger level
  • Saving Throw Proficiencies: Strength, Dexterity
  • Skill Proficiencies: Choose 3: Animal Handling, Athletics, Insight, Investigation, Nature, Perception, Stealth, Survival
  • Weapon Proficiencies: Simple Weapons, Martial Weapons
  • Tool Proficiencies: None
  • Armor Training: Light Armor, Medium Armor, Shields
  • Starting Equipment: Choose A or B: (A) Studded Leather Armor, Scimitar, Shortsword, Longbow, 20 Arrows, Quiver, Druidic Focus, Explorer’s Pack, and 7 GP; or (B) 150 GP

Core Mechanics

Spellcasting

Rangers have access to a limited selection of spells focused on enhancing attacks, mobility, and utility. Their spellcasting supports rather than defines their role.

Favored Enemy / Hunter Features

Rangers gain abilities that improve their effectiveness against certain targets or enhance their combat style, reinforcing their identity as specialists.

Exploration Utility

Many Ranger abilities improve tracking, movement, and interaction with the environment, making them particularly effective outside of combat.

Level 1 Features

  • Favored Enemy — Improved effectiveness against chosen targets
  • Weapon Mastery — Access to specialized weapon techniques

At level 1, the Ranger begins as a capable combatant with a focus on adaptability and specialization.

Playstyle

In Combat

  • Maintain consistent damage over multiple turns
  • Use positioning to control distance from enemies
  • Enhance attacks with spells when needed

Resource Management

  • Spell slots are limited and should support key moments
  • Balance sustained output with occasional boosts

Out of Combat

  • Excels at tracking, navigation, and environmental awareness
  • Provides utility through skills and spells

Subclasses (Ranger Archetypes)

Rangers choose an archetype that defines their hunting style and specialization.

  • Hunter — Focuses on straightforward combat efficiency
  • Beast Master — Gains an animal companion for combat and utility
  • Gloom Stalker — Specializes in ambush and fighting in darkness
  • Fey Wanderer — Gains magical abilities tied to the Feywild

These archetypes shift the Ranger toward either direct combat, tactical play, or more magical and unusual abilities.

Strengths & Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Consistent damage output
  • Strong versatility and adaptability
  • Useful both in and out of combat

Weaknesses

  • Limited spellcasting compared to full casters
  • Less burst damage than some martial classes
  • Can feel unfocused without clear build direction

Building a Ranger

Ability Score Priorities

  • Dexterity — Primary combat stat
  • Wisdom — Supports spells and abilities
  • Constitution — Improves survivability

General Advice

  • Choose a clear combat style early (ranged vs melee)
  • Use spells to enhance your strengths, not replace them
  • Leverage your utility outside combat
