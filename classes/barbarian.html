Barbarian

A primal warrior who relies on raw physical power, resilience, and rage to overwhelm enemies.

Barbarians are frontline combatants built for durability and direct engagement. They excel at absorbing damage and dealing consistent melee attacks, with a straightforward and highly physical playstyle.

Overview

  • Primary Role: Tank / Carry
  • Combat Style: Melee
  • Complexity: Very Low
  • Key Abilities: Strength, Constitution

Barbarians are defined by their ability to endure heavy damage while remaining effective in combat. Their mechanics emphasize simplicity, making them one of the most accessible classes to play.

Core Traits

  • Primary Ability: Strength
  • Hit Point Die: D12 per Barbarian level
  • Saving Throw Proficiencies: Strength, Constitution
  • Skill Proficiencies: Choose 2: Animal Handling, Athletics, Intimidation, Nature, Perception, Survival
  • Weapon Proficiencies: Simple Weapons, Martial Weapons
  • Tool Proficiencies: None
  • Armor Training: Light Armor, Medium Armor, Shields
  • Starting Equipment: Choose A or B: (A) Greataxe, 4 Handaxes, Explorer’s Pack, and 15 GP; or (B) 75 GP

Core Mechanics

Rage

A limited-use state that enhances your combat effectiveness. While raging, you deal increased damage, gain resistance to certain types of damage, and become significantly harder to take down.

Unarmored Defense

Allows you to calculate your Armor Class without wearing armor, using your physical resilience instead. This supports a more mobile and natural combat style.

Reckless Attack

Grants advantage on your attacks at the cost of making yourself easier to hit. This creates a clear risk-reward decision during combat.

Level 1 Features

  • Rage — Enhanced combat state with damage and resistance benefits
  • Unarmored Defense — Alternative Armor Class calculation

From level 1, the Barbarian establishes its identity as a durable frontline combatant capable of sustaining and dealing damage effectively.

Playstyle

In Combat

  • Engage enemies directly in melee range
  • Activate Rage early to maximize its benefits
  • Use Reckless Attack when increased damage is worth the risk

Resource Management

  • Rage has limited uses and should be reserved for meaningful encounters
  • Deciding when to take risks with Reckless Attack is key

Out of Combat

  • Strong in physical challenges and survival situations
  • Limited utility compared to spellcasting classes

Subclasses (Primal Paths)

At later levels, Barbarians choose a Primal Path that defines how their rage manifests.

  • Path of the Berserker — Focused on raw aggression and sustained offense
  • Path of the Wild Heart — Draws on animalistic traits for adaptability
  • Path of the World Tree — Gains control and support abilities tied to cosmic forces
  • Path of the Zealot — Combines rage with divine power and resilience

Each path modifies your role slightly, adding either offensive, defensive, or utility-oriented features.

Strengths & Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Exceptional durability
  • Consistent melee damage
  • Simple and reliable mechanics

Weaknesses

  • Very limited ranged options
  • Low utility outside combat
  • Relies heavily on being in melee range

Building a Barbarian

Ability Score Priorities

  • Strength — Improves attack and damage
  • Constitution — Increases durability and survivability
  • Dexterity — Helps with Armor Class

General Advice

  • Enter Rage early in important fights
  • Stay in melee to maintain effectiveness
  • Use Reckless Attack strategically, not constantly
