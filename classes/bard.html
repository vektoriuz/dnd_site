Bard

A versatile performer who uses magic, inspiration, and skill to influence both allies and enemies.

Bards combine spellcasting, support abilities, and strong utility to adapt to many situations. They are defined by flexibility and timing, often enabling others while still contributing directly through magic and skills.

Overview

  • Primary Role: Support / Control
  • Combat Style: Hybrid (Full Caster + Utility)
  • Complexity: High
  • Key Abilities: Charisma

Bards are defined by their adaptability. They can influence outcomes through buffs, debuffs, and creative spell use, making them effective in both combat and social situations.

Core Traits

  • Primary Ability: Charisma
  • Hit Point Die: D8 per Bard level
  • Saving Throw Proficiencies: Dexterity, Charisma
  • Skill Proficiencies: Choose 3: Any Skills
  • Weapon Proficiencies: Simple Weapons
  • Tool Proficiencies: Choose 3: Musical Instruments
  • Armor Training: Light Armor
  • Starting Equipment: Choose A or B: (A) Leather Armor, 2 Daggers, Musical Instrument, Entertainer’s Pack, and 19 GP; or (B) 90 GP

Core Mechanics

Bardic Inspiration

Allows you to grant bonuses to allies, improving their chances of success. This is a core support mechanic that enhances team performance.

Spellcasting (Known Spells)

Bards know a limited number of spells, focusing on support, control, and utility rather than raw damage.

Jack of All Trades

Grants partial proficiency in many abilities, making the Bard broadly capable across a wide range of tasks.

Expertise

Enhances selected skills, allowing the Bard to excel in key areas.

Level 1 Features

  • Bardic Inspiration — Support allies with bonuses
  • Spellcasting — Access to versatile magic

At level 1, the Bard establishes its role as a support-focused caster with strong influence over outcomes.

Playstyle

In Combat

  • Support allies with Bardic Inspiration
  • Use spells to control or disrupt enemies
  • Adapt actions based on the situation

Resource Management

  • Balance Bardic Inspiration uses with spell slots
  • Choose when to support versus act directly

Out of Combat

  • Excels in social interaction and skill-based challenges
  • Provides utility through a wide range of abilities

Subclasses (Bard Colleges)

Bards join a College that defines how they apply their talents and magic.

  • College of Lore — Focuses on knowledge and spell versatility
  • College of Valor — Combines support with martial capability
  • College of Glamour — Uses charm and presence to influence others
  • College of Dance — Emphasizes movement, rhythm, and positional play

Each College shifts the Bard toward either pure support, hybrid combat, or more specialized control and utility roles.

Strengths & Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Highly versatile and adaptable
  • Strong support and control abilities
  • Excellent utility outside combat

Weaknesses

  • Lower raw damage compared to dedicated classes
  • Requires strong decision-making each turn
  • Limited spell selection compared to prepared casters

Building a Bard

Ability Score Priorities

  • Charisma — Core spellcasting and abilities
  • Dexterity — Improves defense
  • Constitution — Increases survivability

General Advice

  • Think in terms of team impact, not just personal output
  • Use Bardic Inspiration proactively
  • Adapt your actions based on the situation
