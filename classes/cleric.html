Cleric

A divine spellcaster who channels sacred power to heal, protect, and shape the battlefield.

Clerics are highly versatile characters capable of fulfilling multiple roles. They combine durability, support, and powerful spellcasting, adapting their abilities based on preparation and domain choice.

Overview

  • Primary Role: Support / Tank / Control
  • Combat Style: Hybrid (Melee + Full Spellcasting)
  • Complexity: Medium
  • Key Abilities: Wisdom

Clerics are defined by their flexibility. Through prepared spellcasting, they can adjust their role between encounters, making them one of the most adaptable classes in the game.

Core Traits

  • Primary Ability: Wisdom
  • Hit Point Die: D8 per Cleric level
  • Saving Throw Proficiencies: Wisdom, Charisma
  • Skill Proficiencies: Choose 2: History, Insight, Medicine, Persuasion, Religion
  • Weapon Proficiencies: Simple Weapons
  • Tool Proficiencies: None
  • Armor Training: Light Armor, Medium Armor, Shields
  • Starting Equipment: Choose A or B: (A) Scale Mail, Shield, Mace, Holy Symbol, Priest’s Pack, and 7 GP; or (B) 110 GP

Core Mechanics

Spellcasting (Prepared)

Clerics prepare spells from a wide list each day. This allows them to adapt to different situations, but requires planning and understanding of available options.

Channel Divinity

A limited-use ability that provides powerful effects depending on your subclass. These can include healing, damage, or unique utility options.

Divine Domain

Your chosen domain defines your specialization and grants additional abilities and spells, shaping your overall role.

Level 1 Features

  • Spellcasting — Access to divine magic
  • Divine Domain — Choose your specialization

At level 1, the Cleric already gains access to both spellcasting and a defining subclass, making early choices especially impactful.

Playstyle

In Combat

  • Support allies through healing and buffs
  • Control the battlefield with spells
  • Engage in melee when appropriate

Resource Management

  • Spell slots must be allocated carefully
  • Prepared spells determine your available tools
  • Channel Divinity offers powerful but limited effects

Out of Combat

  • Provides healing, utility, and problem-solving tools
  • Can adapt spell choices to anticipated challenges

Subclasses (Divine Domains)

Clerics choose a domain that defines how their divine power manifests.

  • Life Domain — Focuses on healing and support
  • Light Domain — Uses radiant and fire-based damage
  • Trickery Domain — Emphasizes deception and mobility
  • War Domain — Enhances martial combat abilities

Each domain significantly alters your playstyle, shifting emphasis between support, offense, or hybrid combat.

Strengths & Weaknesses

Strengths

  • Highly adaptable spellcasting
  • Strong support and healing capabilities
  • Can function in multiple roles

Weaknesses

  • Requires planning and system knowledge
  • Resource management can be demanding
  • Effectiveness depends on preparation choices

Building a Cleric

Ability Score Priorities

  • Wisdom — Core spellcasting ability
  • Constitution — Improves durability
  • Strength or Dexterity — Depends on combat style

General Advice

  • Prepare spells based on expected challenges
  • Balance healing with proactive impact
  • Choose a domain that matches your preferred role
