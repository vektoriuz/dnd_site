Character Creation Guide

This guide walks you through the process of creating a character step by step. Each stage defines a different aspect of your character — from their origin and identity to their abilities and role in the world.

You can follow the steps in order, or explore each section individually to better understand your options before making decisions.

1. Choose Your Class

Your class defines your primary role, combat style, and core abilities. It determines what you are capable of in both combat and exploration, and shapes how your character grows over time. As such, this choice is easily the most impactful in the character-creation process, at least mechanistically speaking.

Each class provides:

  • A set of starting features at level 1
  • Access to specific abilities or spellcasting
  • A progression path that expands your capabilities

→ Explore Classes

2. Choose Your Background

Your background represents your character’s life before adventuring. It defines their origin, training, and early experiences.

Mechanically, your background provides:

  • Ability score increases
  • Skill proficiencies
  • A starting feat
  • Equipment and flavor elements

This step establishes both your character’s narrative foundation and part of their mechanical identity.

→ Explore Backgrounds

3. Choose Your Species

Your species (formerly race) defines your physical traits and innate characteristics.

This includes:

  • Size and movement speed
  • Special traits (such as darkvision or resistances)
  • Additional abilities tied to your physiology

Unlike earlier versions of the game, species no longer determine your ability score increases — these now come from your background.

→ Explore Species

4. Refine your character’s identity

Your background, species, and personal traits are closely connected. You can develop them together and revisit earlier choices as your character takes shape.

This step is not defined by rules or fixed options. Instead, you shape who your character is beyond their mechanical choices.

This typically includes:

  • Appearance: What your character looks like, including notable features or style
  • Backstory: Key events, upbringing, and experiences that shaped them
  • Personality: Their behavior, values, quirks, and flaws

Aim to create a character that is interesting to roleplay while still fitting into a group. Strong traits and flaws make characters memorable, but they should not disrupt cooperation within the party.

Tip: Start simple. A few clear traits and a basic backstory are enough to begin. You can develop the character further during play.

5. Assign Ability Scores

Ability scores represent your character’s core attributes:

  • Strength — physical power
  • Dexterity — agility and precision
  • Constitution — endurance and resilience
  • Intelligence — reasoning and knowledge
  • Wisdom — perception and awareness
  • Charisma — presence and force of personality

These scores influence nearly every action you take, from combat effectiveness to skill checks.

At this stage, you will:

  • Distribute your ability scores
  • Apply bonuses from your background
  • Determine your modifiers (which affect rolls)

It can be done during the actual session, and in a way that has been agreed upon.

6. Review and Finalize

Once all choices are made, you can review your character as a complete system:

  • Class features define your capabilities
  • Background shapes your foundation
  • Species adds physical traits
  • Ability scores determine effectiveness

At this point, your character is ready to play.

(Optional features such as spells, equipment, and further customization can be explored separately.)

How to Use This Guide

This site is designed as a structured reference system rather than a strict builder.

  • Follow the steps sequentially
  • Explore each category in detail before deciding
  • Compare options across different paths

The goal is to make the decision process clear and transparent, so you understand not just what to choose, but why each choice matters.
