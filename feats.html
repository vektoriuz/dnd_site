Feats

Feats represent specialized abilities that enhance your character’s capabilities. They can grant new options, improve existing ones, or define a particular playstyle.

Feats are divided into four main categories:

  • Origin Feats — gained at character creation through backgrounds
  • General Feats — broadly available improvements and specializations
  • Fighting Style Feats — combat-focused techniques
  • Epic Boon Feats — powerful abilities gained at high levels

Origin Feats

Alert

You gain the following benefits.

  • Initiative Proficiency. When you roll Initiative, you can add your Proficiency Bonus to the roll.
  • Initiative Swap. Immediately after you roll Initiative, you can swap your Initiative with the Initiative of one willing ally in the same combat. You can't make this swap if you or the ally has the Incapacitated condition.

Crafter

You gain the following benefits.

  • Tool Proficiency. You gain proficiency with three different Artisan's Tools of your choice from the Fast Crafting table.
  • Discount. Whenever you buy a nonmagical item, you receive a 20 percent discount on it.
  • Fast Crafting. When you finish a Long Rest, you can craft one piece of gear from the Fast Crafting table, provided you have the Artisan's Tools associated with that item and have proficiency with those tools. The item lasts until you finish another Long Rest, at which point the item falls apart.

Healer

You gain the following benefits.

  • Battle Medic. If you have a Healer's Kit, you can expend one use of it and tend to a creature within 5 feet of yourself as a Utilize action. That creature can expend one of its Hit Point Dice, and you then roll that die. The creature regains a number of Hit Points equal to the roll plus your Proficiency Bonus.
  • Healing Rerolls. Whenever you roll a die to determine the number of Hit Points you restore with a spell or with this feat's Battle Medic benefit, you can reroll the die if it rolls a 1, and you must use the new roll.

Lucky

You gain the following benefits.

  • Luck Points. You have a number of Luck Points equal to your Proficiency Bonus and can spend the points on the benefits below. You regain your expended Luck Points when you finish a Long Rest.
  • Advantage. When you roll a d20 for a D20 Test, you can spend 1 Luck Point to give yourself Advantage on the roll.
  • Disadvantage. When a creature rolls a d20 for an attack roll against you, you can spend 1 Luck Point to impose Disadvantage on that roll.

Magic Initiate

You gain the following benefits.

  • Two Cantrips. You learn two cantrips of your choice from the Cleric, Druid, or Wizard spell list. Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma is your spellcasting ability for this feat's spells (choose when you select this feat).
  • Level 1 Spell. Choose a level 1 spell from the same list you selected for this feat's cantrips. You always have that spell prepared. You can cast it once without a spell slot, and you regain the ability to cast it in that way when you finish a Long Rest. You can also cast the spell using any spell slots you have.
  • Spell Change. Whenever you gain a new level, you can replace one of the spells you chose for this feat with a different spell of the same level from the chosen spell list.
  • Repeatable. You can take this feat more than once, but you must choose a different spell list each time.

Musician

You gain the following benefits.

  • Instrument Training. You gain proficiency with three Musical Instruments of your choice.
  • Encouraging Song. As you finish a Short or Long Rest, you can play a song on a Musical Instrument with which you have proficiency and give Heroic Inspiration to allies who hear the song. The number of allies you can affect in this way equals your Proficiency Bonus.

Savage Attacker

You've trained to deal particularly damaging strikes. Once per turn when you hit a target with a weapon, you can roll the weapon's damage dice twice and use either roll against the target.

Skilled

You gain proficiency in any combination of three skills or tools of your choice.

  • Repeatable. You can take this feat more than once.

Tavern Brawler

You gain the following benefits.

  • Enhanced Unarmed Strike. When you hit with your Unarmed Strike and deal damage, you can deal Bludgeoning damage equal to 1d4 plus your Strength modifier instead of the normal damage of an Unarmed Strike.
  • Damage Rerolls. Whenever you roll a damage die for your Unarmed Strike, you can reroll the die if it rolls a 1, and you must use the new roll.
  • Improvised Weaponry. You have proficiency with improvised weapons.
  • Push. When you hit a creature with an Unarmed Strike as part of the Attack action on your turn, you can deal damage to the target and also push it 5 feet away from you. You can use this benefit only once per turn.

Tough

Your Hit Point maximum increases by an amount equal to twice your character level when you gain this feat. Whenever you gain a character level thereafter, your Hit Point maximum increases by an additional 2 Hit Points.

General Feats

Ability Score Improvement

You gain the following benefit.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase one ability score of your choice by 2, or increase two ability scores of your choice by 1.

Actor

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Charisma score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Mimicry. You can mimic the speech of another person or the sounds made by other creatures. A creature that hears the sounds can tell they are imitations with a successful Insight check.
  • Performance Expertise. You have Advantage on Charisma (Performance) checks when trying to pass yourself off as a different person.

Athlete

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Strength or Dexterity score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Climb Speed. You gain a Climb Speed equal to your Speed.
  • Stand Up. Standing up from Prone costs only 5 feet of movement.

Charger

You gain the following benefit.

  • Charge Attack. If you move at least 10 feet in a straight line toward a target immediately before hitting it with a melee attack, you can deal extra damage or push the target.

Chef

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Constitution or Wisdom score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Cook’s Utensils. You gain proficiency with Cook’s Utensils.
  • Special Food. During a Short Rest, you can cook special food that grants temporary benefits to creatures who consume it.

Crossbow Expert

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ignore Loading. You ignore the Loading property of crossbows.
  • Close Combat. Being within 5 feet of a hostile creature doesn’t impose Disadvantage on your ranged attack rolls.
  • Bonus Attack. When you attack with a one-handed weapon, you can use a Bonus Action to attack with a hand crossbow you are holding.

Crusher

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Strength or Constitution score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Push. Once per turn when you hit a creature with an attack that deals Bludgeoning damage, you can move it a short distance to an unoccupied space.
  • Critical Advantage. When you score a Critical Hit that deals Bludgeoning damage, attack rolls against that creature have Advantage until the start of your next turn.

Defensive Duelist

You gain the following benefit.

  • Parry. When a creature hits you with a melee attack while you are wielding a finesse weapon, you can use your Reaction to add your Proficiency Bonus to your AC against that attack.

Dual Wielder

You gain the following benefits.

  • Dual Weapon Fighting. You can use Two-Weapon Fighting even when the one-handed melee weapons you are wielding aren’t Light.
  • Quick Draw. You can draw or stow two one-handed weapons when you would normally be able to draw or stow only one.
  • Bonus Damage. When you engage in Two-Weapon Fighting, you gain a bonus to damage.

Durable

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Constitution score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Durable Recovery. When you roll Hit Dice to regain Hit Points, you regain additional Hit Points.

Elemental Adept

You gain the following benefits.

  • Element Choice. Choose one damage type: Acid, Cold, Fire, Lightning, or Thunder.
  • Ignore Resistance. Your spells ignore Resistance to the chosen damage type.
  • Damage Consistency. When you roll damage for a spell of that type, you can treat low rolls as higher results.

Fey Touched

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Fey Magic. You learn Misty Step and one additional spell of your choice from a specific list.
  • Free Casting. You can cast each of these spells once without using a spell slot, and you regain that ability when you finish a Long Rest.

Grappler

You gain the following benefits.

  • Advantage. You have Advantage on attack rolls against a creature Grappled by you.
  • Pin. You can attempt to restrain a creature you have Grappled.

Great Weapon Master

You gain the following benefits.

  • Bonus Attack. When you score a Critical Hit or reduce a creature to 0 Hit Points, you can make an additional attack as a Bonus Action.
  • Power Attack. Before making an attack with a Heavy weapon, you can take a penalty to the attack roll to deal extra damage on a hit.

Heavily Armored

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Strength score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Armor Training. You gain training with Heavy Armor.

Heavy Armor Master

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Strength score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Damage Reduction. While you are wearing Heavy Armor, damage from nonmagical attacks is reduced.

Inspiring Leader

You gain the following benefit.

  • Inspiring Speech. After spending time inspiring your allies, you can grant Temporary Hit Points to yourself and others.

Keen Mind

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Intelligence score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Perfect Recall. You can accurately recall anything you have seen or heard recently.
  • Direction and Time. You always know which way is north and the number of hours left before the next sunrise or sunset.

Lightly Armored

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Strength or Dexterity score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Armor Training. You gain training with Light Armor.

Mage Slayer

You gain the following benefits.

  • Disrupt Casting. When a creature within your reach casts a spell, you can make a melee attack against it as a Reaction.
  • Resilience. You have Advantage on saving throws against spells cast by creatures within your reach.
  • Concentration Breaker. When you deal damage to a creature concentrating on a spell, it has Disadvantage on the saving throw to maintain concentration.

Martial Weapon Training

You gain the following benefit.

  • Weapon Proficiency. You gain proficiency with Martial Weapons.

Medium Armor Master

You gain the following benefits.

  • Dexterity Bonus. Wearing Medium Armor doesn’t impose a limit on the Dexterity bonus you can add to your Armor Class.
  • Stealth. Wearing Medium Armor doesn’t impose Disadvantage on Dexterity (Stealth) checks.

Moderately Armored

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Strength or Dexterity score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Armor Training. You gain training with Medium Armor and Shields.

Mounted Combatant

You gain the following benefits.

  • Mounted Advantage. You have Advantage on melee attack rolls against creatures smaller than your mount.
  • Redirect Attack. You can force an attack that targets your mount to target you instead.
  • Evasion for Mount. Your mount gains improved protection against effects that allow saving throws for half damage.

Observant

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Intelligence or Wisdom score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Keen Observation. You gain a bonus to your passive Perception and Investigation scores.
  • Lip Reading. You can understand speech by reading lips if you can see the speaker’s mouth.

Piercer

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Strength or Dexterity score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Damage Reroll. Once per turn when you hit with an attack that deals Piercing damage, you can reroll one damage die.
  • Critical Bonus. When you score a Critical Hit that deals Piercing damage, you roll one additional damage die.

Poisoner

You gain the following benefits.

  • Poison Crafting. You can create potent poisons.
  • Apply Poison. You can apply poison to a weapon or ammunition as a Bonus Action.
  • Ignore Resistance. Your poison ignores Resistance to Poison damage.

Polearm Master

You gain the following benefits.

  • Bonus Attack. When you take the Attack action with a qualifying weapon, you can make an additional attack as a Bonus Action.
  • Opportunity Attack. When a creature enters your reach, you can make an Opportunity Attack against it.

Resilient

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase one ability score of your choice by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Saving Throw Proficiency. You gain proficiency in saving throws using the chosen ability.

Ritual Caster

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ritual Spells. You learn ritual spells from a chosen spell list.
  • Ritual Book. You keep a book containing your ritual spells and can add more to it.

Sentinel

You gain the following benefits.

  • Stop Movement. When you hit a creature with an Opportunity Attack, its Speed becomes 0 for the rest of the turn.
  • Ignore Disengage. Creatures provoke Opportunity Attacks from you even if they take the Disengage action.
  • Protect Ally. When a creature within your reach attacks a target other than you, you can use your Reaction to make a melee attack against that creature.

Shadow Touched

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Shadow Magic. You learn Invisibility and one additional spell of your choice from a specific list.
  • Free Casting. You can cast each of these spells once without using a spell slot, and you regain that ability when you finish a Long Rest.

Sharpshooter

You gain the following benefits.

  • Long Range. Attacking at long range doesn’t impose Disadvantage on your ranged attack rolls.
  • Ignore Cover. Your ranged attacks ignore half and three-quarters cover.
  • Power Shot. Before making a ranged attack, you can take a penalty to the attack roll to deal extra damage on a hit.

Shield Master

You gain the following benefits.

  • Shield Bash. After taking the Attack action, you can use a Bonus Action to shove a creature with your shield.
  • Shield Defense. You can add your shield’s bonus to Dexterity saving throws against effects that target only you.
  • Evasion. When you succeed on a Dexterity saving throw for half damage, you instead take no damage.

Skill Expert

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase one ability score of your choice by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Skill Proficiency. You gain proficiency in one skill of your choice.
  • Expertise. Choose one skill you have proficiency in; your Proficiency Bonus is doubled for checks using that skill.

Skulker

You gain the following benefits.

  • Hidden Sniper. You can attempt to Hide when lightly obscured.
  • Missed Shot. Making a ranged attack while hidden doesn’t reveal your position if you miss.
  • Dim Light Vision. Dim light doesn’t impose Disadvantage on your Perception checks relying on sight.

Slasher

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Strength or Dexterity score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Slow. Once per turn when you hit a creature with an attack that deals Slashing damage, you can reduce its Speed.
  • Critical Effect. When you score a Critical Hit that deals Slashing damage, the target has Disadvantage on its attack rolls for a duration.

Speedy

You gain the following benefits.

  • Speed Increase. Your Speed increases.
  • Difficult Terrain. Moving through difficult terrain costs you less movement.
  • Dash Mobility. You gain additional mobility benefits when you take the Dash action.

Spell Sniper

You gain the following benefits.

  • Cantrip. You learn one cantrip that requires an attack roll.
  • Extended Range. The range of your attack roll spells is doubled.
  • Ignore Cover. Your spell attacks ignore half and three-quarters cover.

Telekinetic

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Telekinetic Power. You gain the ability to move objects or creatures with your mind.
  • Bonus Action Push. You can use a Bonus Action to push or pull a creature.

Telepathic

You gain the following benefits.

  • Ability Score Increase. Increase your Intelligence, Wisdom, or Charisma score by 1, to a maximum of 20.
  • Telepathy. You can communicate telepathically with others within a certain range.
  • Detect Thoughts. You can cast Detect Thoughts without using a spell slot, regaining the ability after a Long Rest.

War Caster

You gain the following benefits.

  • Concentration. You have Advantage on Constitution saving throws that you make to maintain concentration on a spell.
  • Somatic Components. You can perform somatic components of spells even when you have weapons or a shield in your hands.
  • Opportunity Spell. When a creature provokes an Opportunity Attack from you, you can cast a spell instead of making a melee attack.

Weapon Master

You gain the following benefit.

  • Weapon Proficiency. You gain proficiency with a selection of weapons of your choice.

Fighting Style Feats

Archery

You gain the following benefit.

  • Ranged Precision. You gain a bonus to attack rolls you make with ranged weapons.

Blind Fighting

You gain the following benefit.

  • Blindsight. You have Blindsight with a limited range, allowing you to perceive creatures without relying on sight.

Defense

You gain the following benefit.

  • Armor Defense. While you are wearing armor, you gain a bonus to your Armor Class.

Dueling

You gain the following benefit.

  • Single Weapon Fighting. When you are wielding a melee weapon in one hand and no other weapons, you gain a bonus to damage rolls.

Great Weapon Fighting

You gain the following benefit.

  • Damage Reroll. When you roll damage for a melee weapon wielded with two hands, you can reroll low damage dice.

Interception

You gain the following benefit.

  • Damage Reduction. When a creature you can see hits another creature within your reach, you can use your Reaction to reduce the damage.

Protection

You gain the following benefit.

  • Shield Protection. When a creature you can see attacks a target other than you within your reach, you can use your Reaction to impose Disadvantage on the attack roll.

Thrown Weapon Fighting

You gain the following benefits.

  • Draw Weapon. You can draw a thrown weapon as part of the attack you make with it.
  • Damage Bonus. You gain a bonus to damage rolls with thrown weapons.

Two Weapon Fighting

You gain the following benefit.

  • Off-Hand Damage. When you engage in Two-Weapon Fighting, you can add your ability modifier to the damage of the extra attack.

Unarmed Fighting

You gain the following benefits.

  • Unarmed Damage. Your unarmed strikes deal improved damage.
  • Grapple Damage. You can deal damage to a creature you are Grappling.

Epic Boon Feats

Boon of Combat Prowess

You gain the following benefit.

  • Combat Mastery. Your attacks become more reliable, allowing you to turn missed attacks into hits under certain conditions.

Boon of Dimensional Travel

You gain the following benefit.

  • Teleportation. You can teleport a short distance as a Bonus Action.

Boon of Energy Resistance

You gain the following benefit.

  • Energy Resistance. You gain Resistance to one or more damage types.

Boon of Fate

You gain the following benefit.

  • Fate Manipulation. You can add or subtract a value from attack rolls, ability checks, or saving throws.

Boon of Fortitude

You gain the following benefit.

  • Durability. Your Hit Point maximum increases significantly.

Boon of Irresistible Offense

You gain the following benefit.

  • Ignore Resistance. Your attacks ignore Resistance to damage.

Boon of Recovery

You gain the following benefit.

  • Rapid Healing. You regain Hit Points at the start of your turns under certain conditions.

Boon of Skill

You gain the following benefit.

  • Skill Mastery. You gain proficiency and Expertise in additional skills.

Boon of Speed

You gain the following benefit.

  • Increased Speed. Your Speed increases significantly, and you gain additional mobility benefits.

Boon of Spell Recall

You gain the following benefit.

  • Spell Recovery. You can regain expended spell slots under certain conditions.

Boon of the Night Spirit

You gain the following benefit.

  • Shadow Form. You gain abilities related to darkness, invisibility, or incorporeal movement.

Boon of Truesight

You gain the following benefit.

  • Truesight. You gain the ability to perceive things as they truly are within a certain range.
